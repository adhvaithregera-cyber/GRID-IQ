    WHAT IS FORMULA 1?

    Formula 1 is the world's premier single-seater motorsport championship, governed by the FIA. Ten constructor teams each field two drivers, competing across 22 races worldwide in the 2026 season — with Cadillac making it an 11-team, 22-driver grid for the first time since 2016.

    Teams design and build their own cars within strict technical regulations, pushing engineering and driver skill to the absolute limit. The championship awards two titles: the World Drivers' Championship (WDC) and the World Constructors' Championship (WCC).

    RACE WEEKEND FORMAT

    A standard race weekend runs Thursday–Sunday. Some rounds use a shorter Sprint format with a mini-race on Saturday.

    THU
    MEDIA DAY
    Press conferences and paddock access. Teams reveal strategies publicly.
    FRI
    FP1 + FP2 — FREE PRACTICE
    Two 60-minute sessions. Teams test setups, collect data, and evaluate tire compounds.
    SAT
    FP3 + QUALIFYING
    Final practice, then Q1/Q2/Q3 knockout qualifying. Fastest lap in Q3 takes pole position.
    SUN
    RACE DAY
    ~300km race. Mandatory pit stop(s) for tire changes. First car across the line wins.
    ⚡
    SPRINT WEEKEND FORMAT

    Six rounds per season use a compressed Sprint Weekend format. There is only one free practice session — teams must commit to a setup with minimal data, raising the stakes considerably.

    FRI
    FP1 + SPRINT QUALIFYING (SQ)
    One 60-minute practice session, then SQ1/SQ2/SQ3 knockout qualifying that sets the Sprint grid — not the main race grid.
    SAT
    SPRINT RACE + QUALIFYING
    A 100km Sprint Race (≈ 30 min) awards points to the top 8 finishers. Sprint points are separate from and additional to main race points. Then full Q1/Q2/Q3 qualifying sets Sunday's grid.
    SUN
    RACE DAY
    The full GP runs as normal. Sprint results have no bearing on the main race starting order.
    SPRINT POINTS
    P1 8pts · P2 7pts · P3 6pts · P4 5pts · P5 4pts · P6 3pts · P7 2pts · P8 1pt. No points from P9 onward.
    KEY DIFFERENCE
    No second practice means teams race on very little setup data. Tyre management is critical — there are no free practice laps to test compounds before the Sprint.

    KEY TERMS & GLOSSARY
    X MODE
    2026 active aero — flaps open on straights to reduce drag and maximise top speed. Replaces the old DRS system.
    Z MODE
    Active aero flaps close through corners, generating maximum downforce for grip in technical sectors.
    DRS
    Drag Reduction System — the predecessor to X Mode. A movable rear wing flap that opened on straights to aid overtaking.
    PIT STOP
    A tyre change during the race, typically taking 2–3 seconds. Strategy determines the ideal lap to pit.
    UNDERCUT
    Pitting earlier than a rival to gain track position by running faster on fresh tyres while they're still on old rubber.
    OVERCUT
    Staying out longer than a rival to leapfrog them in the pit stop window, especially effective on circuits where track position matters.
    SAFETY CAR
    Deployed after incidents on track. Bunches up the field behind a pace car — often a critical strategic moment.
    VSC
    Virtual Safety Car. Drivers slow to a prescribed delta time without a physical safety car, minimising field bunching.
    POLE / P.P.
    Pole Position — starting at the front of the grid, earned by the fastest time in Q3 qualifying.
    DNF
    Did Not Finish — a race retirement caused by mechanical failure, accident, or driver error.
    WDC / WCC
    World Drivers' Championship / World Constructors' Championship — the two titles awarded at season's end.
    ERS
    Energy Recovery System — harvests kinetic and heat energy under braking, stored and deployed as electric power. In 2026, ERS equals the combustion engine in output.

    2026 TECHNICAL REGULATIONS

    The most transformative ruleset in F1 history — a complete reimagining of power, aerodynamics, and competition. Every team started from scratch.

    POWER
    50/50 ICE-to-ERS split — approximately 1,000 bhp total. Electric motor output now equals the combustion engine for the first time in F1 history.
    X MODE
    Active aero opens automatically on straights — reduces drag and maximises top speed. Deployed in designated zones, replacing the old DRS system entirely.
    Z MODE
    Active aero closes for corners — generates maximum downforce and peak lateral grip. Mastery of the Z/X transition defines fast lap times in 2026.
    CHASSIS
    Smaller, lighter cars. Narrower front wing, revised ground effect floor. New tyre compounds developed specifically for the 2026 regulation package.
    IMPACT
    The regulation reset has reshuffled the competitive order dramatically. Mercedes leads with the most effective W17 interpretation; Red Bull and Aston Martin are adapting hardest to the new power unit demands.
